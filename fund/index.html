02 · What The Bank Will Ask For

You bring

The standard file a lender asks every borrower for.
  • Two years of tax returns and W-2s
  • Your two most recent pay stubs
  • Bank statements showing your cash to close and reserves
  • The lot deed, or the purchase contract if you are buying
  • A photo ID and your credit authorization
  • Your house plans and specifications

Flynn builds for you

The construction side of the file, the part most homeowners get stuck on.
  • The construction budget and cost breakdown the bank draws against
  • The signed construction agreement
  • The project schedule from foundation to certificate of occupancy
  • The scope of work, what is included and what is not
  • A comp and value story to support the appraisal

That second column is where most custom-home loans stall, and it is exactly the part we hand you, ready for the bank. You bring your financials. We build the package that gets drawn against. When you are ready, send us your numbers and we will put the whole file together.
